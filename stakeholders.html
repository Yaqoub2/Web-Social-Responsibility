20% Rule

20% Rule, also known as the "20% time" states that: Employees are encouraged to spend 20% of their work hours, or one day a week, working on projects of their choice, unrelated to their regular job responsibilities.

Productivity by less Productivity:

Google's 20% rule, also known as the "20% time" policy, is an innovation management strategy that allows employees to spend 20% of their work time on projects that interest them, but which may not necessarily fall within their regular job responsibilities. This approach aims to foster creativity, innovation, and employee engagement. The concept behind the 20% rule is deeply rooted in the belief that giving employees the freedom to explore their passions and ideas can lead to significant discoveries and innovations.

Google's 20% rule has been credited with the development of some of its most successful projects, including Gmail, Google News, and AdSense. These projects not only showcase the innovative potential of allowing employees to pursue their interests but also highlight how such initiatives can significantly contribute to a company's success.
